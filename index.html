Electricity in this World

Over the last few centuries, the availability of energy has changed the course of humanity. Not only have new energy sources been discovered — first fossil fuels, then nuclear, hydropower, and now various renewable technologies – but also the amount of energy we can generate and use. Global electricity consumption has continued to go up rapidly at a rate faster than energy consumption. Between 1980 and 2013, the world’s annual electricity consumption rose from 7300 TWh to 22,100 TWh.

As renewable use continues to grow, countries are looking forward to modernize their electricity grid, making it smarter, more secure, and better integrated across regions. Rich countries and businesses have started to invest huge amounts of money into setting up green electricity generation plants. Advocating for renewables, countries can accelerate the transition toward a clean energy future.
